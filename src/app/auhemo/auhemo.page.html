Causes of Hemophilia

As mentioned above, hemophilia is caused by a genetic mutation. The mutations involve genes that code for proteins that are essential in the blood clotting process. The bleeding symptoms arise because blood clotting is impaired.


Hemophilia A is caused by a mutation in the gene for factor VIII, so there is deficiency of blood clotting process factor

 Causes (Cont)

Hemophilia B (also called Christmas disease) results from a deficiency of factor IX due to a mutation in the corresponding gene.
